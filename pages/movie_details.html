Dunkirk
2017 | 12 | 106 minutes
Allied soldiers are surrounded by German forces and trapped on the beaches of Dunkirk during World War II. The film follows three separate storylines: one on land, one at sea, and one in the air.
Over the course of a week, the soldiers desperately try to evacuate the beach, while civilians in small boats attempt to rescue them. The film is a stunning tribute to the bravery and resilience of those involved in the Dunkirk evacuation. Director Christopher Nolan Writer Christopher Nolan Starring Fionn Whitehead | Barry Keoghan | Tom Hardy | Cillian Murphy

Not what I expected...

Reviewed by: colonel_mustard69

Dunkirk is an incredible masterpiece by director Christopher Nolan. This epic war movie is a gripping, intense and emotionally-charged retelling of the evacuation of Dunkirk during World War II. The movie is a tribute to the courage and bravery of the soldiers, who were trapped on the beaches of Dunkirk, waiting for rescue.


The filmâ€™s cinematography is a work of art, and the visuals are stunning. The aerial shots of the Spitfires, the sea and the beaches are breathtaking. The sound design is also excellent, and it adds to the immersive experience of the film. The music by Hans Zimmer is a perfect accompaniment to the intense action scenes.


The performances by the cast are outstanding, especially considering that the film is largely dialogue-free. Tom Hardy, Kenneth Branagh, Mark Rylance, and Harry Styles give convincing and emotional performances that help to elevate the film beyond just a typical war movie.

Very Nolanesque

Reviewed by: brit-buffer_4u

Dunkirk, directed by Christopher Nolan, is a visually stunning film that tells the story of the evacuation of British soldiers during World War II. While the film has its moments of tension and excitement, it falls short in its character development and storytelling. The film focuses more on the spectacle of the evacuation rather than the personal stories of the soldiers, leaving the audience feeling emotionally disconnected from the characters.


The sound design, while impressive, is often overpowering and can be overwhelming at times, detracting from the overall experience. The non-linear structure of the film also adds confusion and can make it difficult to follow the timeline of events.


Despite its flaws, Dunkirk is still an impressive technical achievement, with breathtaking cinematography and visual effects. However, it ultimately lacks the emotional depth and character development needed to make it a truly great war film. While it may be worth a watch for its technical achievements, it falls short in terms of storytelling.

Absolute masterclass

Reviewed by: chris_N_indaflesh

Dunkirk is a cinematic masterpiece that leaves you breathless from start to finish. Director Christopher Nolan brings to life the story of the evacuation of British soldiers during World War II with stunning visuals and heart-stopping action.


The film is a testament to the human spirit and the courage of those who fought to survive against all odds. The sound design is exceptional, immersing you in the chaos of war and adding to the tension and drama of the story.


The performances are outstanding, particularly by newcomer Fionn Whitehead, who carries the weight of the film on his shoulders. Dunkirk is a must-see film that will leave you in awe of the bravery and sacrifice of those who fought for their country.
